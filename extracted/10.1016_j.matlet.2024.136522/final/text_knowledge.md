### Material System
- Fe-28Mn-10Al-1C-3Cu austenitic low-density steel (3Cu steel).
- Fe-28Mn-10Al-1C austenitic low-density steel (0Cu steel, used for comparison).

### Processing Route and Variables
- Solution treatment: 1000°C for 1 h.
- Aging treatment: 550°C for 3 h.

### Microstructure and Phase Evolution
- After aging at 550°C for 3 h, the 3Cu steel contains two distinct nanoscale precipitates within the austenitic matrix:
    - κ-carbide (a = 0.3775 nm).
    - Cu-rich phase (FCC structure, a = 0.3652 nm).
- The Cu-rich phase exhibits an irregular oval morphology with a diameter of ~10 nm and a small amount of bar shapes with a length of ~30 nm.
- The crystal plane spacing (002) of the Cu-rich phase is 0.182 nm, consistent with FCC Cu.
- The lattice parameter of the austenitic matrix in the solution-treated condition is a = 0.3689 nm.

### Processing-Structure-Property Chain
1.  **Initial State**: Solution treatment at 1000°C for 1 h results in a single-phase austenitic matrix.
2.  **Aging at 550°C for 3 h**:
    - κ-carbide precipitates first from the austenitic matrix, excluding Cu atoms. This leads to Cu enrichment in the adjacent austenitic matrix.
    - The increased Cu concentration near the κ-carbide promotes the heterogeneous nucleation of the FCC-structured Cu-rich phase.
3.  **Coherent Precipitation**: Both κ-carbide and Cu-rich phase maintain a cube-on-cube orientation relationship with the austenitic matrix: [110]γ // [110]κ // [110]Cu and (111)γ // (111)κ // (111)Cu.
4.  **Lattice Misfit & Interfacial Energy**:
    - Misfit (κ-carbide/γ) = 2.3%
    - Misfit (Cu-rich/γ) = 1.0%
    - Misfit (κ-carbide/Cu-rich) = 3.4%
    - Interfacial energy (κ-carbide/γ) = 0.025 J/m²
    - Interfacial energy (Cu-rich/γ) = 0.086 J/m²
    - All misfits are below 5%, indicating coherent interfaces. The low interfacial energy of κ-carbide with the matrix explains its preferential precipitation.
5.  **Result**: A coherent, co-precipitated nanostructure is formed. The Cu-rich phase precipitates within the austenitic matrix rather than on κ-carbide due to its lower lattice misfit (1.0% vs 3.4%) with the matrix, leading to a lower nucleation energy barrier.

### Mechanistic Interpretation
- **Co-precipitation Mechanism**: Cu addition promotes κ-carbide precipitation. Subsequently, κ-carbide precipitation forces Cu atoms into the surrounding austenite, locally increasing Cu concentration and triggering Cu-rich phase nucleation near the κ-carbide.
- **Phase Selection**: κ-carbide precipitates first because its interfacial energy with the austenitic matrix (0.025 J/m²) is lower than that of the Cu-rich phase (0.086 J/m²).
- **Nucleation Site**: The Cu-rich phase nucleates in the austenitic matrix (not on κ-carbide) because the lattice misfit with the matrix (1.0%) is lower than with κ-carbide (3.4%), providing a lower nucleation energy barrier.

### Key Quantitative Findings
- **Lattice Parameters**: κ-carbide (a=0.3775 nm), Cu-rich phase (a=0.3652 nm), γ-matrix (a=0.3689 nm).
- **Lattice Misfits**: κ-carbide/γ = 2.3%; Cu-rich/γ = 1.0%; κ-carbide/Cu-rich = 3.4%.
- **Interfacial Energies**: κ-carbide/γ = 0.025 J/m²; Cu-rich/γ = 0.086 J/m².
- **Cu-rich Phase Size**: Irregular oval diameter ~10 nm; bar length ~30 nm.
- **Cu-rich Phase d-spacing**: (002) plane = 0.182 nm.

### Visual Evidence
- **Figure 1**: HRTEM image and SAED patterns of aged 3Cu steel. SAED patterns confirm the cube-on-cube orientation relationship: [110]γ // [110]κ // [110]Cu and (111)γ // (111)κ // (111)Cu. The SAED pattern in the red border indexes the Cu-rich phase spots (111, 111̄, 200) as a BCC structure, directly supporting the crystallographic orientation relationship between all three phases.
- **Figure 2**: TEM-EDS mapping and HRTEM of aged 3Cu steel.
    - **Figure 2(b) EDS mapping**: Shows Fe, Mn, Al enrichment (bright contrast) in the κ-carbide zone (white dashed box) and Cu depletion there. Conversely, the Cu-rich phase region (magenta area) shows Cu enrichment and Fe, Mn, Al depletion. This directly supports the mechanism that κ-carbide excludes Cu, enriching Cu in the adjacent austenitic matrix to nucleate the Cu-rich phase.
    - **Figure 2(c) and 2(d) HRTEM**: Shows an irregular oval Cu-rich phase (~10 nm diameter) and a spherical Cu-rich phase with a measured lattice fringe of 0.182 nm for the (002) plane, confirming its FCC Cu structure.
